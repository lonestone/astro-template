This is a sample blog post. Everything you see (headings, paragraphs, lists, code blocks, callouts) comes from a single MDX file in `src/content/blog/<slug>/<lang>.mdx`.

## What lives in a blog post

Each post has a small front matter block at the top of the file:

```yaml
---
title: "..."
summary: "..."
date: 2026-04-01
image: ./cover.jpg
author: Your name
similarPosts:
  - other-slug
---
```

The `summary` is shown on the index, next to the card image. The `similarPosts` list surfaces cross-links at the bottom of the article.

## Rich content, zero imports

Any Astro component in `src/components/` is available directly inside MDX. You can drop in a callout:

<Callout type="tip" title="Callouts">
  This block was written with `<Callout type="tip" title="Callouts">` inside the MDX file.
</Callout>

…or a CTA banner, a split section, a YouTube embed, or anything else.

## Table of contents

Every H2 in the article automatically becomes an item in the table of contents on the right. On mobile, the TOC collapses into a dropdown so it stays out of the way.

## Next steps

Duplicate this file, change the slug (the folder name), and you have a new post. Use the sidebar on the right to navigate. Happy writing!
